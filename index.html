Causes of Teenage Pregnancy

Lack of Comprehensive Sex Education

Without proper knowledge of contraception and sexual health, teens are more likely to engage in unprotected sex.

Sexual Abuse and Exploitation

Victims of abuse or exploitation are at a significantly higher risk of becoming pregnant at a young age.

Early Exposure to Sexual Contents

Exposure to sexualized media can influence teens to engage in sexual activities without understanding the risks.

Peer Pressuring

Teens may feel pressured by friends or partners to engage in sexual activities to fit in or prove maturity.

FACTS ABOUT TEENAGE PREGNANCY IN THE PHILIPPINES

The teenage pregnancy rate in the Philippines remains one of the highest in Southeast Asia, with thousands of adolescents giving birth each year. Contributing factors include lack of comprehensive sex education, limited access to contraceptives, poverty, and cultural norms that discourage open discussions about reproductive health. According to government data, the number of births among girls aged 10-19 has decreased in recent years due to awareness campaigns and policy interventions, but it remains a significant public health concern. Early pregnancy often leads to school dropouts, economic struggles, and health risks for both mother and child. Addressing this issue requires continuous efforts in education, healthcare access, and community support.
